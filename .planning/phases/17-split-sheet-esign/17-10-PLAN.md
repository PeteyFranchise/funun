<objective>
De-DocuSeal the artist-facing surface (ESIGN-18, ESIGN-19, P17-10). The provider gate confirmed the integration works; it also confirmed that everything the collaborator touches is currently provider-branded — the invite email, the sender address, and the completion certificate. This plan builds the two Funūn-owned artifacts that replace them: a Funūn-branded signature-invite email sent through the existing Resend integration, and a Funūn Certificate of Completion that cites DocuSeal as the signing provider rather than hiding it.

Purpose: A split sheet is the collaborator's own legal record, often their first contact with Funūn (P17-06 — this is the stickiness thesis applied to collaborators). Handing them a third party's email and a third party's certificate wastes that moment and confuses whose record it is. The free measures land first: disabling the provider's invite email costs nothing, and Funūn's own certificate is just a renderer. The paid white-label of the embed itself is a separate, purchased prerequisite (see below).

Honesty constraint (non-negotiable, and the reason the certificate has a designed type rather than a flat props bag): DocuSeal's audit log captures per-signer IP address, session id, user agent, timezone, and email-verification status. Funūn did not capture any of it. The certificate may report those facts and must attribute them; it must never present them as evidence Funūn independently gathered. This is enforced structurally — the renderer's input separates what Funūn observed from what the provider reported, so an implementer cannot accidentally print the latter as the former.

Pro-plan prerequisite (NOT this plan's work — do not attempt any purchase): white-labeling the embedded form itself, removing the provider's mark from the signing surface, requires the DocuSeal Pro plan at 20 USD per user per month plus 0.20 USD per completion — which matches AM-1's assumed economics exactly. Separately, the sandbox banner observed at the provider gate means Pro is required before ANY real artist use, white-label or not. Pete purchases this; it is recorded in 17-06's user_setup as a blocking dashboard step.

Output: The Funūn signature-invite email module and the Funūn Certificate of Completion renderer, both pure, both credential-free, both tested — ready for 17-06's mint route and 17-07's completion webhook to call.
</objective>

<threat_model>
## Trust Boundaries

| Boundary | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Funūn → collaborator inbox | An outbound message asking someone to sign a legal document; its sender identity and its only link are the recipient's sole trust signals. |
| provider audit data → Funūn artifact | Facts Funūn received rather than observed, rendered onto a document that may be relied on as evidence. |
| collaborator reply → monitored mailbox | A collaborator's only channel to a human when something is wrong with a document they are being asked to sign. |

## STRIDE Threat Register

| Threat ID | Category | Component | Severity | Disposition | Mitigation Plan |
|-----------|----------|-----------|----------|-------------|-----------------|
| T-17-30 | Repudiation | certificate implying Funūn captured provider evidence | high | mitigate | Provenance is split at the type level; the providerReported group has exactly one consumer (the attributed provenance section) and the attribution sentence naming DocuSeal is test-asserted (P17-10 honesty constraint). |
| T-17-31 | Spoofing | signature-invite email mistaken for phishing, or a real phish mimicking it | high | mitigate | A single consistent sender (ESIGN_FROM_EMAIL) whose domain deliverability was confirmed at the provider gate; exactly one action link, always to Funūn's own approve page for that signer's token; no provider domain in the message to muddy the trust signal; reply_to is a real monitored mailbox so a suspicious recipient can reach a human. |
| T-17-32 | Information Disclosure | one signer's invite exposing another party's private data | medium | mitigate | The invite names the parties and the recipient's own share — data already visible to every party on the sheet via the existing approval flow — and carries only the recipient's own token; no other signer's token, contact detail, or provider identifier is included. |
| T-17-33 | Denial of Service | a failed invite send aborting a paid mint | medium | mitigate | sendSignatureInvite never throws and returns a structured per-signer result; the envelope is already spent by the time invites go out, so the caller reports partial delivery and offers a resend rather than losing the mint. |
| T-17-34 | Information Disclosure | ESIGN_FROM_EMAIL unset causing a silent fallback to the generic sender | low | mitigate | The wrapper's explicit-from gating turns an unset value into a structured no-op rather than a fallback send; asserted in tests. |
| T-17-SC | Tampering | npm/pip/cargo installs | low | accept | No package installs in this plan. Resend and @react-pdf/renderer are existing project dependencies. |
</threat_model>
