<objective>
Rebuild the split-sheet document from a table into an agreement (ESIGN-16, P17-09), and gate its operative language behind attorney review (ESIGN-17, P17-09a). The 2026-07-20 provider gate executed a real 3-signer test against the current renderer and the finding was blunt: the artifact is a table, not an instrument. It has one SPLIT column, no scope statement, no date, no publisher, no legal names, no sample disclosure, no ISWC/ISRC, and no operative language. A row reading "Producer — 25%" on that document is genuinely ambiguous between a publishing share, a master share, and producer points, and the parties signing it are agreeing to different things.

Purpose: 17-06 mints live envelopes against whatever this renderer produces. Whatever ships here is the document real artists sign and rely on for PRO, MLC, and CWR registration downstream. Fixing the shape after signatures exist is not possible — a signed instrument cannot be retroactively clarified. This plan therefore lands the data model, the document, and the counsel gate together, before the first live mint.
